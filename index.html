Two things sink a short-term let

One bad arrival outweighs fifty good stays

A hair in the shower. An unmade bed. No toilet roll. The guest photographs it, the review sits on your listing, and your ranking drops. You can't delete it and you can't argue with it. Every changeover we do ends with the property checked room by room against how it should look.

Damage claims fail without proof

A guest breaks something and your claim goes nowhere — not because it didn't happen, but because you can't show what condition the property was in before they arrived. Most owners lose damage claims on evidence, not on truth. After every changeover, you hold dated, time-stamped photographs of every room.

What we do between checkout and check-in

A changeover is not a quick wipe-round. This is the list we work through on every single visit, in the window between guests.

  • Every room cleaned in full — bedrooms, living space, kitchen, bathroom
  • Floors vacuumed and mopped throughout
  • Beds stripped and used linen bagged for laundry
  • Beds remade with fresh linen, towels set out
  • Kitchen: surfaces, sink, hob and microwave cleaned, fridge checked and cleared
  • Bathroom: shower, screen and taps cleaned and dried, sanitaryware disinfected
  • Consumables restocked — toilet roll, soap, bin liners, dishwasher tablets, tea and coffee
  • Bins emptied; recycling out on collection day
  • Furniture, cushions and kit reset to match your listing photos
  • Anything broken, worn or missing flagged to you the same day
  • Every room photographed, and the time-stamped report sent before the next guest arrives

Every visit ends with evidence

Before we leave, we photograph every room. You get a room-by-room record — dated, time-stamped, with anything that needs your attention flagged: a mark on a wall, a loose hinge, sealant starting to fail.

If a guest claims the property wasn't clean on arrival, you can show that it was. If a guest damages something, you have dated evidence of its condition before they walked in. Claims are usually lost for lack of proof of prior condition — the report is that proof, built up visit after visit.

The report is included in every changeover, at every level. It is not an extra and we never skip it.
